The Problem

F&I Gross Is Leaking
on Every Single Deal

Stores are leaving six figures — sometimes far more — on the table annually — not from lack of effort, but from not having the right tool at the desk.

```
01
Reserve Is Calculated Wrong
Every lender calculates reserve differently — some pay at buy rate, some only on markup, some pay a flat percentage of amount financed regardless of rate. Your dealer agreement determines which method each bank uses. Most F&I managers use a rough estimate or skip the math entirely. The error compounds across every deal.
02
Wrong Lender Gets Called First
Submitting to the wrong bank means stips, counters, and deals that lose momentum or fall apart. Every incorrect first call costs time, urgency, and gross. There is no system telling you who should be first — until now.
03
No Institutional Memory
When a lender tightens overlays on a profile type, nobody logs it. Every F&I manager makes the same mistake on their own — sometimes for months. The knowledge lives in one person's head and walks out the door with them.
04
Programs Change. Nobody Knows.
Rate sheets update constantly. A lender quietly raises their LTV cap, tightens a FICO floor, or changes their reserve grid. F&I managers keep using stale data — sometimes by months — while their store loses deals it should be winning.
```
